Branding · Personal Project · 2019
Project background
How about a thrilling watter battle?
Myutt-Nori is a project based on the scenario of "If Songkran Festival is held in Korea". "Myutt" means korean traditional chess pieces for a game that people enjoy on Korean NewYear's Day. "Nori" means a game. Songkran Festival is also the NewYear's Day inThailand. Myutt — Nori was inspired by these two things in common. Myutt — Nori is played against the backdrop of Nami Island in Korea, and the island itself becomes a huge Myutt — Nori board (Chess board).Through this project, I would like to resolve the difference in the level of cultural exchange between the two contries.
Content & Visual Inspitation
Myutt — Nori's content were inspired by the first official diplomatic relationship between Korea andThailand when Thailand sided with Korea in the war. Motifs of the three elements, soldiers' camoflague patterns, festival-goers, and splashed water were used to produce graphic types.
Admission Bracelet
Myutt — Nori's entry bracelet contains the main graphic of the festival, as well as the roles and blood types of participants. This was based on information in the military uniform.
Emblem & Patches
Participants in Myutt — Nori are given their own roles.This role can be distinguished by attaching an emblem patch to the festival's costume, Panchow. Alternatively, a special emblem patch is given to participants when they achieve significant achievements in Myutt — Nori.
Brochure
Myutt — Nori's brochure contains basic rules and information about the festival, and maps to locate it. It can be used as a shield to defend against water attacks as it is covered by vinyl.
Posters
Myutt — Nori's poster emphasizes the entertainment and fun of the festival in the form of a logo play.
